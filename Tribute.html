Guido van Rossum

The Creator of Python

  • 1956 - Born in Haarlem, Netherlands on Janruary 31st.
  • 1982 - Received a master's degree in mathematics and computer science from the University of Amsterdam.
  • 1986 - Wrote and contributed a glob() routine to BSD Unix and helped develop the ABC programming language.
  • 1989 - Van Rossum, who had been looking for a "hobby" programming project, decided to write an interpreter for a "new scripting language" which would become Pyton.
  • 1999 - Submitted a funding proposal to DARPA called "Computer Programming for Everybody", in which he further defined his goals for Python:
    • ~An easy and intuitive language just as powerful as major competitors.
    • ~Completely so anyone can contribute to its development.
    • ~Code that is as understandable as plain English.
    • ~Suitability for everyday tasks, allowing for short development times.
  • 2002 - Received the 2001 Award for the Advancement of Free Software from the Free Software Foundation (FSF) for his work on Python.
  • 2003 - Received an NLUUG award for his contributions to UNIX.
  • 2006 - Recognized as a Distinguished Engineer by the Association for Computing Machinery.
  • 2013 - Began working at the cloud file storage company Dropbox.
  • 2018 - Made a 'Fellow' of the Computer History museum.

In the Python community, Van Rossum is known as a "Benevolent Dictator For Life", though he is simply known as "the perfect troll" to his co-workers at Dropbox.

You can find out more about Van Rossum on his github.io page and more about Python by visiting the official blog.
